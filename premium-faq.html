About Our Matcha

What makes ceremonial-grade matcha different?
Ceremonial-grade matcha is made from the youngest, most tender tea leaves picked during the first harvest. These leaves are shade-grown for 29 days, hand-selected, deveined, destemmed, and stone-milled into a fine powder. The result is vibrant green color, smooth texture, natural sweetness, and minimal bitterness.
Where does your matcha come from?
All our premium matcha is sourced directly from family-run tea gardens in Uji, Kyoto‚Äîthe birthplace of Japanese tea cultivation. We partner with renowned producers including Marukyu Koyamaen (founded 1688), Ippodo Tea (300+ years), and Horii Shichimeien (400+ years).
How should I store matcha?
Store unopened tins in a cool, dark place. Once opened, keep in the refrigerator in an airtight container and consume within 4-6 weeks for optimal freshness. Always use a dry scoop to prevent moisture contamination.
What's the difference between your matcha grades?
Haru: Our best-seller, perfect balance of sweetness and umami. Ideal for beginners and lattes.
Hikari: Bold, complex flavor with rich umami. For experienced matcha enthusiasts.
Mie: Daily blend, versatile for lattes and smoothies (coming soon).
Akari: Ultra-premium single cultivar reserve (coming soon).
